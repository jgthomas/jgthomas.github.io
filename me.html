My pivot

This site is the record of my pivot from academic to software engineer.

Two things happened in early 2017 that I didn't think were connected: I realised the academic world was not for me, and I felt the need for a new interest—a creative outlet of some kind.

I thought back to the programming I did during the first years of my PhD. I had forgotten pretty much everything, except that I really enjoyed it. So I started playing with code again. This rapidly became a runaway obsession, which I have since ridden through another degree, volunteer work, and into my new professional life.

I could definitely have been here earlier. As a child I played with Visual Basic, getting up early to shift elements around on the family computer. Somehow, though, it didn't 'stick', that first time round. It has been my fortune, therefore, to pick up a childlike obsession with programming and computer science as an adult, with all the advantages and disadvantages that come with that timing.

So, you could say I'm something of a “late developer”...but I'm here now!
